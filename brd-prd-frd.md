# Project Requirements Documents: BRD, PRD, & FRD

A **BRD**, **PRD**, and **FRD** are essential for defining a project and ensuring everyone is aligned. 

They follow a logical order, moving from high-level business goals to detailed technical specifications.

---

## 1. Business Requirements Document (BRD)

The **BRD** comes first. It's a high-level document that defines the **"why"** and **"what"** from a business perspective. Its audience is primarily business stakeholders and executives.

* **Core Purpose:** To justify the project and align it with business goals.
* **Key Components:**
    * **Business Objectives:** The overarching goals you aim to achieve (e.g., "increase customer retention by 15%").
    * **Project Scope:** What's in and out of the project.
    * **Success Metrics:** How you'll measure success (e.g., specific KPIs).

---

## 2. Product Requirements Document (PRD)

The **PRD** translates the BRD's business needs into specific product features. It focuses on the **"what"** from a product and user perspective and is the core guide for the product, design, and development teams.

* **Core Purpose:** To define the product's features and functionality.
* **Key Components:**
    * **User Personas & Stories:** Who the user is and what they need to accomplish.
    * **Product Features:** A prioritized list of what the product will do.
    * **User Flow:** A visual or descriptive map of how a user navigates the product.

---

## 3. Functional Requirements Document (FRD)

The **FRD** is the most technical document. It takes the PRD's features and outlines the **"how"**—how the system will behave to meet those requirements. This document is mainly for the engineering and QA teams.

* **Core Purpose:** To provide detailed technical specifications for development.
* **Key Components:**
    * **Detailed System Behavior:** The exact action the system performs in response to user input or a condition.
    * **Data Models:** How data is structured and stored.
    * **System Interactions:** How different parts of the system communicate with each other.

---

## The Project Flow

Think of the documents as a funnel from broad to specific:

1.  **BRD:** The high-level business **"why"** and **"what."**
2.  **PRD:** The detailed product **"what."**
3.  **FRD:** The technical **"how."**

Following this order ensures that the technical work is always aligned with a clear business objective. Does seeing it in this format help clarify the roles of each document?
